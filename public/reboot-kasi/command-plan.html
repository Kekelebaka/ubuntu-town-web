01 — The Operating Insight

The spaza doesn't close. It converts — through the runtime we already run.

The Great Township Opportunity Reset is the umbrella; Reboot Kasi is its first national deployment — the proving ground for Ubuntu Town OS, the Township Opportunity Operating System, at national scale. Everyone else is fighting over who owns the shop. We do something the OS was built for: turn a real township signal into coordinated, proof-backed work. Reboot Kasi is not a new system — it is the biggest workpack campaign Ubuntu Town has ever run.

How a conversion maps to the runtime

Signal: a vacant or struggling spaza + a coordinator on the ground. → Opportunity: "convert this shop into an opportunity node." → Workpack: the Convert-a-Spaza steps, rules & proof requirements. → Proof: owner onboarded, Opportunity Point live, first KasiBuy parcel collected. → Review: coordinator + Kopano check evidence, a human approves. → Memory: the town learns which conversions stick, and the next one gets easier.

Because it rides the existing runtime, every conversion strengthens town memory, coordinator trust, and the national activation numbers already on ubuntutown.co.za (50 towns, 500+ coordinators, 5,000+ workpacks for 2026).

03 — The Coordinator Workpack

"Convert-a-Spaza" — the playbook every coordinator runs

One repeatable workpack, run on WhatsApp, tracked in the enter portal, assisted by Kopano, approved by a human. The offer to the owner is the whole game: you keep your shop and your customers — we add new income streams, tools and a gateway to the nodes.

Identify

Coordinator logs a candidate spaza (vacant, struggling, or willing) as a Signal with a photo + GPS.→ Signal captured

Assess

Kopano scores readiness (footfall, space, power, owner intent); coordinator confirms tier fit.→ Opportunity, human-confirmed

Pitch the offer

Keep your shop + add: KasiBuy parcels, community-services commissions, optional paid corner, free training, gateway to the nodes.→ Workpack opened

Onboard

Light KYC via the PWA; owner becomes an Opportunity Point; signage + starter kit issued.→ Assignment active

Activate

First parcel collected, first community service booked, corner (if Tier 2/3) switched on.→ Proof submitted

Prove & remember

Coordinator + Kopano review evidence; a human approves; the win feeds town memory + the counter.→ Review › Memory

Why an owner says yes

No displacement, no takeover. The owner keeps the shop and gains 3–6 new income streams (parcel fees, community-services commissions, corner revenue, referral fees into the nodes) plus buying power through KasiBuy group orders — the exact edge foreign-owned bulk-buying networks have and independents lack.

The compounding move

A Tier-1 spaza is a doorway: once it's an Opportunity Point + KasiBuy point, adding an AI Café corner, a Job Fixer kiosk, an Ubuntu Health screening day or an Academy class is a small next step — each an opportunity mapped into a new workpack. One converted shop can, over time, touch every cluster.

Day 05 — The money layer stays invisible

Ubuntu Town is not a bank or a fintech product. Payments and settlement are back-end infrastructure — plumbing the nodes run on, never a public-facing brand. The only money-facing node the public ever sees is Ubuntu Finance — financial literacy, tax, bookkeeping and savings education. Everything else about how money moves stays invisible, on purpose.

HubWorks — the ecosystem's scale engine

HubWorks is the biggest job multiplier in the ecosystem — a single site scales 10 → 50 → 200 seats of employment as demand proves out. It's contract-gated, so it isn't the fast lane; AI Café + KasiBuy stay the fast-deployment anchors for the 31 July target while HubWorks builds the flagship employment capacity behind them.

08 — Metrics & Governance

Count what's real; protect the mission

Conversion & quality

Live counter by tier & town; % of Tier-1 shops still transacting after 14 days (stickiness, not just sign-ups).

Jobs & income

New income streams per owner; youth (under-35) hired per flagship node; community-services volume per town.

Proof integrity

Every conversion carries evidence, human-reviewed. Kopano recommends; a person approves. No proof, no count.

Mission integrity

Zero displacement (vacant/willing shops only); political neutrality upheld; zero cash-request/patronage incidents logged.
