How it runs

Five steps. One loop. No signal gets lost.

Every part of Ubuntu Town — every pathway, every Reboot Kasi node — runs through the same runtime. It's the operating system underneath the town, not a new app on top of it.

01 · Signal

Someone speaks up

A resident, business, or coordinator reaches out to Ubuntu Town — a need, an idea, a problem, a shop standing empty.

02 · Opportunity

Kopano scopes it

Kopano reads the signal, classifies it, and drafts what the opportunity could look like — recommendation only, never final say.

03 · Workpack

A human assigns it

A coordinator approves the scope and packages it into a workpack — the unit of real, paid, trackable work.

04 · Proof

Work gets verified

Photos, sign-off, delivery confirmation — proof is captured at source, reviewed by a human before it counts.

05 · Memory

The town remembers

Every proof becomes permanent town memory — reputation, history, and the evidence base for the next opportunity.

🤝

Kopano is governed AI — always.

Kopano can explain, draft, classify, and recommend at every step of the loop. It never publishes, approves, or governs alone. Humans keep the pen — coordinators, town partners, and sponsors review and sign off on everything that becomes memory.
